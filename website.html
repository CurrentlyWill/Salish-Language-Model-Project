Salish Language Model Project

University of Washington | AIS 499 | March 2024

William Warren - Graduate

Abstract

This project outlines the development of a Large Language Model (LLM) aimed at enhancing translation capabilities and educational resources for the Salish language. The undertaking involved a meticulous approach to data collection, dictionary creation, and strategic adaptations to improve translation accuracy.

Language is a living expression of culture, and by preserving the Salish language, we safeguard the rich traditions and values embedded within it. Each word carries the weight of our history and identity, making our efforts to revitalize and teach the language crucial in protecting our cultural heritage.

Project Phases

Phase Description
Phase I Foundation and preparation, focusing on data collection and dictionary creation.
Phase II Initial fine-tuning efforts to enhance model accuracy using Salish-English datasets.
Phase III Transitioning to advanced models for improved translation and synthetic data creation.
Phase IV Experimentation with embeddings to refine translation capabilities.
Phase V Fine-tuning with high-quality data to enhance the model's performance.
